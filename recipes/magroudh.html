Magroudh

Magroudh is a traditional pastry that hails from North African countries like Morocco, Algeria, and Tunisia. It is a popular and cherished treat in these regions. Magroudh is made by enclosing date paste in a dough, which is then fried until golden brown. It is often drizzled with honey and garnished with sesame seeds. This sweet and delectable pastry represents the rich culinary heritage of North Africa.

Ingedients:
  • 2 cups semolina
  • 1/2 cup fine-grain durum wheat flour
  • 1/2 cup melted butter or vegetable oil
  • 1/4 cup orange blossom water
  • 1/4 cup water (approximately)
  • Date paste (for filling)
  • Vegetable oil (for frying)
  • Honey (for drizzling, optional)
  • Sesame seeds (for garnish, optional)

Steps:
  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the semolina, durum wheat flour, and melted butter or vegetable oil. Mix well until the mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  2. Gradually add the orange blossom water while mixing with your hands. Add water, a little at a time, until the dough comes together and is smooth and pliable.
  3. Divide the dough into small portions and roll each portion into a ball.
  4. Flatten each dough ball with your hands to form a small circle.
  5. Place a small amount of date paste in the center of each dough circle.
  6. Fold the dough over the date paste to enclose it completely, shaping it into a small cylindrical or crescent shape. Press the edges to seal.
  7. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan or pot for frying. The oil should be hot but not smoking.
  8. Carefully place the shaped pastries into the hot oil and fry until they turn golden brown. Flip them occasionally to ensure even browning.
  9. Once cooked, remove the pastries from the oil and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  10. Optional: Drizzle the Magroudh pastries with honey and sprinkle sesame seeds on top for added flavor and garnish.
  11. Allow the pastries to cool before serving.
